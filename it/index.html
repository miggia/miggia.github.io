About Me

I’ve loved making things for as long as I can remember. I work to design high performance mechanisms with complex behaviours yet minimal embodiments, exploiting manufacturing constraints to obtain high efficiency and reduced costs.

Questa pagina presenta una panoramica del mio lavoro degli ultimi anni.

Breve Biografia

Nel 2006 ho conseguito la Laurea Magistrale con lode in Ingegneria Meccanica dall’Università degli Studi di Modena e Reggio Emilia. Mi sono poi trasferito a Genova dove ho iniziato il dottorato di ricerca all’Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia (IIT). In IIT ho lavorato allo sviluppo hardware del robot umanoide iCub con Lorenzo Natale, Giorgio Metta and Giulio Sandini.

Dopo aver conseguito il dottorato sono rimasto in IIT, dove ho assunto la guida dello sviluppo hardware del progetto iCub, come PostDoc a partire dal 2010 e come Tecnologo dal 2016. Nell’agosto del 2019 sono diventato il coordinatore della facility IIT per la progettazione e la costruzione di sistemi meccanici.

Progetti

N-UU mechanisms

Given their large, singularity-free workspace, 2DOF mechanisms of the N-UU class are extremely promising for use in robotic wrists. This explores possible solutions to be employed for the iCub forearm-hand assembly.

R1

The goal of the R1 project was to bring the technology developed in the iCub project one step closer to the market. This problem was addressed with a design-for-manufacturing approach, novel solutions for robot mechanisms, and by a wide use of polymeric materials. The first complete robot prototype was demonstrated in just 16 months from the beginning of the project.

iCub expressive face

This result of this project, carried out jointly with Prof. Gerard Bailly and Dr Frederic Elisei at the University of Grenoble, was the creation of an articulated talking face developed. The head featured an articulated jaw, four independent lip movements and was covered by a specially designed elastic tissue.

iCub SEA legs

This project, carried out in tight collaboration with colleagues of the Advanced Robotics department, allowed the improvement of the iCub legs sub-assembly and the addition of series elastic actuators (SEA) to the main leg joints.

iCub2

Initial testing of the iCub platform highlighted the need for further improvements. This project addressed the most important limitations by completing the second design iteration of the iCub humanoid.
