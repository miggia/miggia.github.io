R1

Jan 1, 2015
Video

The years 2015 and 2016 were entirely devoted to a new project, strongly endorsed by the scientific direction of IIT. The ambitious aim of the project was to design, build and demonstrate a robot functionally equivalent to the iCub humanoid but at one-twentieth (1⁄20) of the cost.

This goal was pursued with a design-for-manufacturing approach, novel solutions for robot mechanisms, and by a wide use of polymeric materials.

The project was of a rather large scale, involving over 30 people working in 7 different institutions in different geographical locations (Italy, Spain, Switzerland and New Zealand). The total budget of the project was around 1.1M€ (including personnel, consumables, and external services).

During this period, I managed the project and acted as the main reference person for all development related issues. As part of my project management duty, I introduced an AGILE working methodology, which largely increased the efficiency and proficiency of the development team. I personally designed several robot sub-assemblies. Over the course of 16 months, we developed from scratch three new types of robotic actuators and a novel motor control methodology.

The first prototype of the R1 robot was demonstrated in April 2016. The main results of this project were published in:

Parmiggiani, A., Fiorio, L., Scalzo, A., Sureshbabu, A. V., Randazzo, M., Maggiali, M., … Metta, G. (2017). The design and validation of the R1 personal humanoid. In IEEE/RSJ Int. Conf. on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS). DOI: 10.1109/IROS.2017.8202224
